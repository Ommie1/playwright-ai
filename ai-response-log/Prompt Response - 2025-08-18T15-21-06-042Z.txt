How can I renew my Emirates ID?

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Renew Your Emirates ID

Renewing your Emirates ID is a straightforward process, but it’s important to follow each step carefully to avoid delays or fines. Below is a clear, step-by-step guide based on the latest official sources.

1. When to Renew
You can apply for renewal up to 6 months before your Emirates ID expires.
You must renew within 30 days after expiry to avoid late fines (AED 20 per day) 3.
2. Prepare Required Documents

The documents you need depend on your status (UAE citizen, GCC national, or expatriate resident). Generally, you will need:

Your current Emirates ID card
Valid passport
Valid UAE residence visa (for expatriates)
Recent passport-sized photo (if required)
For children: birth certificate and parents’ documents

Check the full list for your category on the official ICP website or service portal 1.

3. Submit Your Renewal Application

You can apply through one of the following channels:

The ICP (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Ports Security) website: 2
ICP mobile app (available on Google Play, App Store, and Huawei)
Accredited typing centers
4. Pay the Renewal Fees
The fee depends on the validity period and your status.
Standard renewal fee: AED 270 (for most cases)
Additional service charges may apply if using typing centers or urgent services 4.
5. Biometric Data (If Required)
Applicants above 15 years old may need to visit an ICP Customer Happiness Centre to update biometrics (fingerprints and photo).
You will receive an SMS if this step is required.
6. Track Your Application
After submitting your application, you will receive an SMS with your application status.
You can also track your application online via the ICP website.
7. Collect Your New Emirates ID
Once your ID is ready, you will receive an SMS notification.
Collect your card from the designated Customer Happiness Centre or arrange for home delivery via courier.
8. Special Notes
If you are outside the UAE, Emiratis can now renew their Emirates ID online without returning to the UAE.
Expatriates must have a valid UAE residence visa to renew their Emirates ID.
If your ID is lost or damaged, report it and apply for a replacement immediately.
